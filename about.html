WHO ARE THE FORGOTTEN?

The homeless,the less fortunate, the slum dwellers. Those often left out of environmental decisions, yet most affected by pollution.

WHAT IS POLLUTION?

Pollution is when harmful things like smoke, waste, and plastic damage the air, water, or land.

TYPES OF POLLUTION

  • Air Pollution

    • Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the air that can adversely affect human health, the environment, and the climate.

  • Water Pollution

    • Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies, such as rivers, lakes, and oceans, with harmful substances that degrade water quality and pose risks to human health and the environment.

  • Land Pollution

    • Land pollution refers to the contamination of the Earth's land surface due to human activities, resulting in harmful effects on the environment and public health.

  • Noise Pollution

    • Noise pollution is the presence of unwanted or excessive sound that can have harmful effects on human health, wildlife, and environmental quality.

HOW ARE THE FORGOTTEN AFFECTED?

  • Air Pollution

    • -They live close to factories, roads, or burning garbage sites.

    -They breathe in dirty air and that may leading to health complications e.g asthma.

    -They can’t afford masks, medicine, or clean indoor air

  • Water Pollution

    • -They use dirty rivers or wells for drinking, washing, or cooking.

    -They are prone to diseases like cholera, typhoid, and diarrhea.

    -They hav no access to water filters or safe bottled water.

  • Land Pollution

    • -They live near dumpsites or in areas with uncollected garbage.

    -They play or sleep around waste, broken glass and even toxic materials.

    -They risk getting cuts, infections or other long-term diseases from exposure.

  • Noise Pollution

    • -They are affected by constant loud noise from traffic, factories, or busy neighborhoods.

    -They have no quiet places to rest or study.

    -The exposure to lud noises can cause stress, sleep problems, and hearing issues.

    WHAT CAN WE DO?

    • To Help Reduce Air Pollution

      • -Plant more trees and help preserve them in poor communities to clean the air

      -Avoid burning garbage and teach about safer waste disposal

      -Donate masks or support free medical checkups

    • To Help Reduce Water Pollution

      • -Help clean local water bodies

      -Support or create safe water stations and teach about the different ways of purifying water

      -Teach about not dumping chemicals or waste into water

      -Collect and reuse rainwater where possible

    • To Help Reduce Land Pollution

      • -Start or support recycling programs

      - Organize clean-up days with youth/street kids

      -Provide means of waste diposal and management e.g providing garbage bins

      -Advocate for proper garbage collection

    • To Help Reduce Noise Pollution

      • -Support city rules that limit loud noise at night

      -Use soft horns or keep music low in public areas

      -Help create quiet safe spaces for rest and learning

      -Raise awareness about the problems excessive/loud noises causes
